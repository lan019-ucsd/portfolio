Project 2: Persuasive or Deceptive Visualization?

Laura Ngo - lan019@ucsd.edu

As abortion clinics shut down, residents face increased travel requirements for care, widening disparities in access to reproductive healthcare across the U.S.

Clinic Closures Increase Travel and Access Inequality

Design Decisions and Rationale:

  • Z-Score Standardization (Score: 2): Transformed raw travel percentages into z-scores to standardize how each state differs from national average, allowing for apparent and comparible patterns across regions. This transformation accounts for variability and remains earnest given its strengthening influence on the map's analytical integrity.
  • Binned Color Categories (Score: 1): I created travel categories based on standard deviation thresholds to facilitate readability from a non-statistical audience. This binning transforms continuous z-scores into discrete levels to quickly identify varying access inequalities.
  • Continuous Color Scale (Score:0.5): This color scale enforces the emotional weight of inequality. Darker hues signal greater barriers to abortion access as more residents must travel out-of-state for abortion care. This color choice is intentionally persuasive as the color intensity can correspond to severity.
  • Title/Subtitle (Score 2): The title neutrally retains factual accuracy in detailing the inequality of reproductive access states in 2020. The subtitle also includes general date transformations and a simple explanation of color hues (z-score) to guide the viewer's interpretation toward the proposition's stance.
  • Legend(Score 1): We use the the Z-score with additional descriptive terms to allow readability in both statistical and plain language and allow the map to be intuitively and emotionally resonant.

Regions with Major Clinic Losses Correspond to Decreasing Abortion Rates

Design Decisions and Rationale:

  • Regional Data Aggregation (Score: 2) I grouped all states into the main four U.S Cenus regions. Then, I computed the mean percentage change for abortion rates and clinic numbers to simplify state-level variation into smaller, more easily-interpretable categories. This decision reduces noise from regional disparities and preserves earnest directional trends for regional disparities.
  • Category Color Encoding (Score: 1) I used a conventional and intuitive color scheme to distinguish between clinic access (blue) and abortion rate (red). Although the color contrast may heighten emotional impact, this decision directly represents two distinct categories.
  • Bar Annotation (Score: 2) Each bar is annotated with its corresponding percentage change to ensure accessible numerical precision and reduce potential misinterpretation. In other words, this enhancees transparancy as it emphasizes quantitative support.
  • Title/Subtitle (Score: 1) The title conveys the main argument and what the visualization shows, though the framing is somewhat persuasiave as it guides interpretation towards the decline of abortion rates. The subtitle properly outlines and clarifies data transformations.
